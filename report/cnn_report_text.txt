\subsection{1D-CNN model}

For our part of the project, we implemented a 1D convolutional neural network for time-series prediction on the Jena Climate dataset. The goal was to predict the target variable $T$ (degC) from multivariate historical measurements.

The dataset was first preprocessed by keeping only numeric columns and converting the original 10-minute sampling into hourly sampling by taking every 6th row. After preprocessing, the data had shape $(70092, 14)$. The target variable was standardised together with the input features. We used a chronological split into training, validation, and test sets in order to avoid data leakage.

We tested two CNN configurations and two window sizes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Config A: $32 \rightarrow 64$ channels, kernel sizes $3$ and $3$, dropout $0.2$
    \item Config B: $64 \rightarrow 128$ channels, kernel sizes $5$ and $3$, dropout $0.3$
    \item Window sizes: $24$ and $48$
\end{itemize}

Training used Adam optimizer with learning rate $10^{-3}$, batch size $64$, maximum $50$ epochs, and early stopping with patience $10$.

The final test results were:

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\hline
Model & Window & MAE [$^\circ$C] & RMSE [$^\circ$C] & $R^2$ \\
\hline
CNN Config A & 24 & 1.172568 & 1.528655 & 0.961460 \\
CNN Config B & 24 & 0.785000 & 1.009769 & 0.983183 \\
CNN Config A & 48 & 0.691050 & 0.908385 & 0.986413 \\
CNN Config B & 48 & 0.660662 & 0.866241 & 0.987644 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\caption{Final CNN results on the test set}
\end{table}

The results show that both a larger model and a longer input window improved prediction quality. The best model was CNN Config B with window size 48, which achieved the lowest MAE and RMSE and the highest $R^2$. Therefore, this configuration was selected as the final CNN solution for comparison with the other approaches in the project.

For the CNN part, the final selected model is Config B with input window size 48, because it achieved the best overall prediction accuracy on the Jena Climate test set.
